### Case Study on Phishing

#### What is phishing?

Phishing is when fraudsters or people with malicious intent pretend to be a trusted entity ( bank, government, etc). to trick people into revealing sensitive information.

#### How it Happens in India?

##### Fake messages/emails
  you might get a message stating, " Your bank account will be blocked !! click here to update your KYC immediately", the link might look authentic and real but is actually fake and is used to steal your identity.

##### Fake Portals and websites
  This is often overlooked as creating a website has become far too easier than ever and this type of phising is on the rise. These are often the most authentic looking as well, replicating the real website one-to-one and any info typed in,  is gone straight to the phishers

##### Whatsapp phishing
  As Whatsapp became the coutnrywide communication network, it has also become a popular medium for phishers to steal data.


Moreover, based on the 2025 Study , India is globally the second largest target for phishing scam, and the first in the APJ zone (Asia-Pacific-Japan). 

This is more apparant with a failed Phishing attack that had gone viral on the X Platform, where the target was called multiple times from the scammer to get his credentials faking to be a person from Bank giving away money as a random lottery. 

The victim was smart enough to stop it the right time realising that he was a scammer but this incident is not alien as the scammer himselfs berates the victim with the bold statement that they scam thousands and losing him wouldn't affect them. Sadly the bold statement is true as millions actually do fall for it, and it's not just individuals as private firms fall for it as well.
